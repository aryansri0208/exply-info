Use Cases

Exply offers different modes to help you understand text in various ways. Choose the mode that best fits what you're trying to accomplish.

Explain

Understanding

For understanding complex or unfamiliar text

Explain mode provides clear, contextual explanations without oversimplifying. It helps you grasp the meaning of unfamiliar terms, concepts, or complex statements while maintaining the depth and nuance of the original text.

When to use it

When you encounter technical jargon, academic terminology, specialized concepts, or complex sentences that you want to understand better.

What problem it solves

Breaking down complex ideas into understandable explanations without losing the original meaning or context.

How it helps

You get immediate clarification without having to leave the page or search for definitions elsewhere. The explanation appears right where you're reading, keeping your flow intact.

Great for:

  • Research papers and academic articles
  • Technical documentation and API references
  • Scientific articles and medical content
  • Legal documents and policy texts
  • Any content with specialized terminology

Simplify

Clarity

Rewrites complex language into plain, easy-to-understand terms

Simplify mode takes dense, complicated text and rewrites it using simpler language while keeping the original meaning intact. It transforms academic, legal, or technical prose into everyday language that's easier to digest.

When to use it

When text is written in overly complex or formal language, making it difficult to follow even if you understand the concepts.

What problem it solves

Makes dense, verbose, or unnecessarily complicated writing accessible without losing the core message or meaning.

How it helps

You can quickly understand the essence of complex passages without getting bogged down by intricate sentence structures or specialized vocabulary.

Great for:

  • Dense academic writing
  • Legal contracts and terms of service
  • Technical specifications
  • Complex policy documents
  • Verbose or overly formal content

"So what?" (Implications)

Analysis

Explains why a piece of text matters

This mode goes beyond what the text says to explain why it matters. It highlights implications, consequences, practical significance, and real-world relevance of the information you're reading.

When to use it

When you understand what the text says but want to know why it's important, what it means in practice, or how it affects you or others.

What problem it solves

Bridges the gap between factual information and practical understanding, helping you see the bigger picture and real-world consequences.

How it helps

Enables deeper analysis and better decision-making by connecting abstract information to concrete implications and outcomes.

Great for:

  • News articles and current events
  • Research findings and studies
  • Business and financial reports
  • Policy changes and announcements
  • Analysis and opinion pieces

Translate

Language

Translates selected text while preserving context and meaning

Translate mode produces natural, readable translations rather than literal word-for-word output. It understands context, idioms, and nuance to deliver translations that feel natural in the target language.

When to use it

When you encounter text in a language you're not fully comfortable with, or when you need to understand content in multiple languages.

What problem it solves

Provides context-aware translations that capture meaning, tone, and nuance rather than producing awkward literal translations.

How it helps

You can read and understand content in different languages without losing the original meaning or context, making multilingual research and reading seamless.

Great for:

  • Multilingual reading and research
  • International news and articles
  • Foreign language documentation
  • Academic papers in other languages
  • Understanding content while learning a language
